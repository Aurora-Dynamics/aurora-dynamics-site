About Aurora Dynamics

Built on a simple observation

The sales–marketing gap in life science isn't a communication problem. It's a project problem. More meetings don't fix it. Shared work does.

The gap nobody talks about honestly

In biotech, the gap between sales and marketing isn't news. Everyone knows it's there. Sales thinks marketing is disconnected from the market. Marketing thinks sales ignores everything they produce. Leadership tries to solve it with alignment meetings, shared dashboards, and the occasional off-site.

None of it works — because the problem isn't how the two teams communicate. It's that they don't work on the same things.

Marketing builds campaigns that sales doesn't use. Sales generates leads that marketing can't nurture. And somewhere in between, prospects fall through the cracks. Not because anyone dropped the ball, but because it was nobody's ball to begin with.

Shared projects change the dynamic

Aurora Dynamics exists because of a straightforward idea: if you give sales and marketing a shared deliverable to build together, they start thinking like one team — without anyone telling them to.

When a sales rep helps shape the messaging in a nurture sequence, they're invested in it. When marketing builds a LinkedIn content framework that the BD team actually uses, they see the impact directly. When both teams track the same email course sign-ups as a KPI, the "us vs them" dynamic starts to dissolve.

This isn't about theory. It's about giving both teams something concrete to point to and say: "We built that together, and it's working."

How we work

Principles, not buzzwords

Clear beats clever

In everything — copy, strategy, communication. If it doesn't make immediate sense to both a sales rep and a marketing manager, it's not ready.

Both teams, every project

We don't build things for marketing or for sales. Every deliverable is designed to be used by both — and is better because both contributed to it.

Sequence over ambition

Do one thing well before adding the next. We help companies build their pipeline in the right order — LinkedIn first, then lead capture, then nurture — so nothing is wasted.
