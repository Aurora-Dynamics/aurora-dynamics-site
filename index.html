The pattern

Everyone works in silos. It's how we've all been trained.

Marketing builds campaigns. Sales works their contacts. Both teams are busy, but neither sees what the other is doing. Prospects slip through the space between them.

This is how almost every biotech company operates. The default. But the companies pulling ahead have figured out something straightforward: when both teams work on the same deliverables, the pipeline stops leaking.

Our approach

What changes when both teams build together

Most marketing deliverables live on one side of the fence. A blog post belongs to marketing. A sales deck belongs to sales. Neither team touches the other's work, so neither team benefits from the other's perspective.

We build things that sit in the middle. LinkedIn content that trains sales to think like marketers. Email courses that give marketing a tool sales actually shares. Nurture sequences with messaging both teams shaped.

The result: shorter sales cycles, fewer cold prospects, and a pipeline both teams can see working.
