## 2. Brand Voice & Tone

**Primary qualities:** Clear, human, warm, consultative.
**When it matters:** Confident — but never a know-it-all.

### Voice principles

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Speak plainly and directly | Use jargon to sound impressive |
| Be warm and approachable | Be overly casual or jokey |
| Show confidence through specificity | Make sweeping claims or absolutes |
| Acknowledge complexity honestly | Oversimplify to seem clever |
| Guide — like a trusted advisor | Lecture — like a professor |
| Say "here's what I'd recommend" | Say "you need to" or "you should" |

### Tone spectrum

```
Casual ←――――●―――→ Formal
              ↑
        Slightly left of centre.
        Professional but never stiff.
```

**Guiding rule:** Clear beats clever in all communication.

### Color usage rules

- **Primary backgrounds are light.** The site is predominantly warm white (#f7f5f2) with the illustration style providing visual energy.
- **Dark sections are used sparingly** — for emphasis moments like the hero, CTA blocks, or footer. Use Deep Charcoal (#2a3132) for these.
- **Aurora Blue is the dominant brand color.** Use it for headlines, links, and primary buttons.
- **Biotech Green supports, never leads.** Use for accents, secondary elements, and to echo the logo gradient.
- **Warm Amber is reserved for action.** Only CTAs and key interactive elements — its scarcity is what makes it effective.
- **Never use pure white (#ffffff) or pure black (#000000).** The palette is warm and human, not sterile.

### Logo gradient reference

The Aurora Dynamics logo mark uses a gradient from blue (#5c6baf region) through teal to green (#72b452 region). This gradient can be used sparingly as a decorative element but should not be overused — let the logo own it.

## 4. Typography

The typographic pairing is editorial in character — a distinctive serif for headings paired with a clean, warm sans-serif for body text. This creates contrast with the expressive illustration style: the typography stays composed while the artwork brings the energy.

| Role | Font | Fallback | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Headings | **Fraunces** | serif | Variable optical size serif. Warm, characterful, editorial. Use semibold (600) or bold (700). Italic for emphasis moments in headlines. |
| Body | **DM Sans** | sans-serif | Clean, geometric, highly readable. Warm without being soft. Use regular (400) and medium (500). |
| Accents/labels | **DM Sans** | sans-serif | Uppercase, letter-spaced (0.1em) for labels, tags, and small UI text. Weight 600. |

### Typography rules

- Headings should be large and confident but not shouty. Let whitespace do the work.
- Use italic Fraunces sparingly in headlines for emphasis — e.g. a key phrase or emotional beat.
- Body copy at 16–18px minimum for readability.
- Line height of 1.5–1.6 for body text, 1.15–1.2 for headings.
- Limit to two font families maximum across the entire site.
- The serif headings create an editorial, trusted-advisor feel — this is intentional and should not be replaced with a sans-serif.

## 5. Design Language

### Illustration style (established with designer)

The brand has an existing illustration style created by a specialist designer. Key characteristics:

- **Mixed media feel:** Hand-drawn ink work combined with watercolor textures, fine geometric line art, and splatter/particle effects.
- **Scientific subjects treated artistically:** Biological imagery (cells, organisms, DNA, molecular structures) rendered with creative flair rather than clinical accuracy.
- **Layered and dynamic:** Elements overlap, splash outward, and create a sense of motion and energy.
- **Transparent backgrounds:** Illustrations are designed to sit on light surfaces. The textures and ink work create their own contrast.
- **Dual palette within illustrations:** Primarily uses cool blues and greys, with fine line work in darker tones. When used in Aurora Dynamics context, these should be adapted to align with brand colors where possible.
- **Hexagonal motifs:** Geometric hexagons appear as structural elements, nodding to molecular/scientific structures.

### How the illustration style informs the website

- **Section dividers and decorative elements** can borrow the flowing line/wave mesh patterns and subtle particle effects.
- **Hero and feature sections** can incorporate illustration assets directly as visual anchors.
- **Background textures** can use very subtle grain or watercolor washes at low opacity to add warmth and depth.
- **The overall feel should be:** a clean, professional layout with moments of artistic energy — not a flat corporate site, not an art gallery. The illustrations bring the personality; the layout gives them room to breathe.

### Layout principles

- Generous whitespace — don't crowd the illustrations or the message.
- **Editorial feel** — the overall aesthetic should feel like a quality publication. Restrained, refined layout with expressive illustration as the visual counterpoint.
- **Light hero sections** preferred over dark — this lets illustrations show their transparent textures. Dark sections reserved for CTA blocks and footer.
- **Pill-shaped buttons** (large border-radius) for primary and secondary CTAs — softer and more approachable than sharp rectangles.
- **Column dividers** over card borders where appropriate — cleaner, more editorial.
- Full-width hero and CTA sections with contained content widths for readability.
- Gradient accent bars (using the aurora gradient) as subtle visual markers on cards or section elements — 40px wide, 3px tall.
- Subtle shadows over hard borders where depth is needed.

- Do not stretch, rotate, or recolor the logo.
- The logo mark (the aurora triangle) can be used alone as a favicon or social media icon.
- Do not place the light logo version on light backgrounds or the dark version on dark backgrounds — use the appropriate variant.

## 7. Photography & Imagery Direction

When stock photography or additional imagery is needed:

- **Prefer real, human moments** over staged corporate shots. People in conversation, working at whiteboards, at conferences.
- **Biotech context is a plus** — labs, events, product launches — but avoid clichéd "scientist with pipette" imagery.
- **Warm lighting and natural tones** to match the brand palette.
- **No heavy filters or overlays.** Let photos breathe. A subtle warm tone grade is fine.
- When illustrations and photography coexist, give each its own space — don't overlay illustrations on photos.
